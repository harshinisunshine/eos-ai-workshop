The Random Forest algorithm is like a "wisdom of the crowd" for data. Instead of relying on a single Decision Tree—which can be prone to making errors or getting too caught up in specific details (overfitting)—Random Forest builds a whole forest of trees. Each tree is trained on a slightly different, random subset of the data and uses a random selection of features. When it’s time to make a prediction, every tree in the forest "votes," and the algorithm picks the most popular answer. This teamwork makes the model much more stable and accurate than any individual tree could be on its own.
